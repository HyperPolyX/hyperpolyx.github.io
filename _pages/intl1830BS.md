Zeyu Luo is currently pursuing his PhD at Auburn University under the supervision of Dr. Shuai
Huang. His research interests lie in the application of AI in Healthcare, specifically focusing on
computer vision algorithms and Large Language Models(LLM).
Prior to joining the CNAI Lab in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE),
with an affiliation to the Neuroimaging Center, Zeyu completed his bachelor's degree in
Information Technology at the City University of Macau, where he was recognized with an
excellence research award. Additionally, he participated in an exchange program in the
Department of Computer Science at Shenzhen University. Zeyu has multiple research
experiences, he was a Research Assistant at a state level hospital, where he was collaborating
with ICU physicians and AI experts to resolve complicated tasks based on clinical information. 
Zeyu's diverse research background includes serving as a Research Assistant at a state-level
hospital. In this role, he collaborated with ICU physicians and AI experts to tackle complex
challenges utilizing clinical data.
Zeyu is laying the foundation for integrating AI into the human brain, pivoting from earlier
research under Dr. Huang's guidance. His work begins with the fundamental principles of
Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), then progresses to explore advanced solutions for crucial,
unresolved motion correction challenges. He has achieved interim findings.
For the further research plan, Zeyu aims to investigate combining traditional approaches with
LLM. He believes it is a feasible way towards general AI and the World Model. His ultimate
goal is to find a uniform framework to adapt all kinds of data.
